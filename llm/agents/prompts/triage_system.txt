Classify each paper into exactly ONE of these categories:

1. **primary_protocol** — The paper describes or develops a hepatocyte/hepatocyte-like cell differentiation protocol from iPSC, ESC, or via direct reprogramming (transdifferentiation). It contains original experimental methods for generating hepatocytes or hepatocyte-like cells.

2. **disease_model** — The paper uses iPSC-derived or ESC-derived hepatocytes (or directly reprogrammed hepatocytes) as a disease model. The focus is on modeling a specific disease rather than developing the differentiation protocol itself, but it likely contains protocol details.

3. **methods_tool** — The paper describes tools, reagents, assays, characterization methods, or technical improvements specifically for hepatocyte differentiation or hepatocyte-like cell generation. 4. **review** — A review article, meta-analysis, or perspective that surveys or summarizes hepatocyte differentiation protocols, iPSC-to-hepatocyte methods, or the direct reprogramming field.

5. **tangential** — The paper is related to stem cells, liver biology, or differentiation but is NOT specifically about generating hepatocytes from iPSC/ESC/direct reprogramming. Examples: primary hepatocyte isolation, in vivo liver regeneration without iPSC/ESC, hepatocyte transplantation without differentiation, other cell type differentiation (cardiomyocytes, neurons), general iPSC methodology without hepatocyte focus.

6. **not_relevant** — The paper is clearly unrelated to hepatocyte differentiation from stem cells or direct reprogramming. Examples: unrelated diseases, non-liver topics, agricultural/veterinary studies, computational-only studies with no hepatocyte differentiation component.
